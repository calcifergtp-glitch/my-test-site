Air Fryer vs Convection Oven: Which is Better?

Published 2025-08-31

Cooking Method

Air fryers circulate hot air around food using a fan, creating a crispy exterior similar to frying.

Convection ovens also use a fan to circulate hot air but generally have a larger cooking space, suitable for bigger meals.

Cooking Time and Efficiency

Air fryers typically cook food faster due to their concentrated cooking space and high heat.

Convection ovens may take longer but can cook multiple dishes at once due to their larger capacity.

Versatility

Air fryers excel at frying, roasting, and baking smaller portions.

Convection ovens are more versatile for baking, roasting, and broiling larger meals.

Health Considerations

Air fryers use significantly less oil than traditional frying methods, making them a healthier option.

Convection ovens also reduce oil usage compared to conventional ovens, but not as dramatically as air fryers.

FAQ

Can I use aluminum foil in an air fryer?

Yes, but ensure it doesn't block airflow or touch the heating element.

What is the average price difference between the two?

Air fryers typically range from $50 to $300, while convection ovens are usually $100 to $500.
